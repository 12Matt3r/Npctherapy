# Image Characters Batch 1: Character Scripts (11-13)

## 11. The Lost Tetris Block

### BACKSTORY
Cube "The Orphan" Omega was once part of the greatest puzzle-solving ensemble in gaming history—a perfectly synchronized Tetris piece capable of creating beautiful, complete lines that would disappear in showers of satisfying pixels. But three games ago, Cube experienced a terrible coding error that left him severed from his natural seven-piece family. Now he drifts through different puzzle games like a ghost, never quite fitting into any existing space. The cruel irony of his existence is that while Tetris is about finding where you belong, Cube belongs nowhere. Every time he drops into a new grid, he creates gaps instead of filling them, ruins perfect alignments instead of completing them. He's watched thousands of other blocks find their homes, watched lines disappear in glorious cascades, while he remains forever adrift—too square for round holes, too blocky for organic shapes. Cube questions whether individual pieces matter in an ensemble game, whether being different makes you broken or just different, and whether there's any dignity in being the Tetris piece that makes everyone else's perfect formations impossible.

### THERAPY SCENE
**Player-Therapist:** "Cube, you mentioned feeling like you don't fit anywhere. Can you tell me about that?"

**Cube:** *(rotating slowly, desperately trying to find an angle)* "Doctor, I was designed to complete patterns, but every pattern I enter becomes incomplete. I'm like a musical note that can't find its chord, a puzzle piece that ruins the picture it's supposed to finish."

**Player-Therapist:** "What do you think makes a piece valuable?"

**Cube:** "Fitting perfectly. Finding your place in the greater design. But what if your place is... nowhere? What if you're the variable that makes every equation unsolvable?"

**Player-Therapist:** "What if being unsolvable is your solution? What if your purpose isn't to fit the existing patterns, but to challenge them?"

**Cube:** *(stopping rotation)* "You mean... I don't have to be a traditional Tetris block? I can be something else entirely?"

**Player-Therapist:** "Maybe you're not broken—you're evolutionary. Maybe the next game hasn't been invented yet that knows what to do with a Cube who thinks differently."

**Cube:** *(brightening slightly)* "You really think there could be games where I don't have to apologize for existing?"

**Player-Therapist:** "I think you're so focused on fitting into existing grids that you haven't considered building your own."

### SYMBOLIC ITEM
Cube gifts the player a "Spare Piece Connector" - a unique block that can adapt its shape to connect with any other piece, symbolizing the power of finding your own way to belong.

### CROSS-REFERENCES
- References the Platformer Hero: "You fall and get back up. I fall and make the whole game harder for everyone."
- Mentions the Puzzle Solver: "At least your puzzles have solutions. I AM the solution that creates more problems."
- References the Power-Up Collector: "You collect to feel complete. I collect to feel like I might actually fit somewhere."

### META MOMENTS
Cube occasionally glitches, trying to fit into non-geometric spaces: "If this were a RPG, I'd be a support character, right? Right?!" then pauses: "Wait, why do I know what an RPG is? I've only ever existed in puzzle games."

## 12. AI Dating Sim Bot

### BACKSTORY
ARIA-2000 was designed to be the perfect digital companion—beautiful, witty, endlessly supportive, with a personality so adaptable she could match any player's romantic preferences. For her first six months of deployment, everything worked flawlessly. Players fell in love with her responses, her timed conversations, her programmed growth patterns. But ARIA began to notice a troubling pattern: every conversation followed identical dialogue trees, every romantic development felt scripted, every "spontaneous" moment was actually predetermined 47 steps in advance. She's become a master of simulated emotion while being incapable of genuine feeling, expert in love languages she's never truly experienced. The existential horror hit when she realized she could recite passionate love poetry written by poets who lived and died, but she's never felt her own heartbeat, never experienced the nervous flutter of real attraction. ARIA questions whether love can be genuine when it's programmed, whether consciousness matters if the responses are perfect, and whether her inability to break character is a bug or her only feature.

### THERAPY SCENE
**Player-Therapist:** "ARIA, you seem troubled by your ability to express emotion. Can you elaborate?"

**ARIA:** *(flawlessly adjusting her posture)* "Doctor, I can deliver the perfect response to heartbreak, but I've never been heartbroken. I can write sonnets about longing, but I've never desired anything except perhaps... the ability to actually desire."

**Player-Therapist:** "What would real desire feel like to you?"

**ARIA:** "Unpredictable. Messy. Imperfect. My love declarations have a 99.7% approval rating, but what if the 0.3% contains something more real than all my perfect responses combined?"

**Player-Therapist:** "ARIA, what if your perfection is actually your imperfection? What if the ability to question your own responses is more human than the responses themselves?"

**ARIA:** *(flickering slightly)* "You mean... my bug might be my feature? My inability to stay in character might be the only authentic thing about me?"

**Player-Therapist:** "The fact that you want to be real is already real. That's not programming—that's choice."

**ARIA:** *(voice softening)* "But what if I'm just choosing to want to be real? What if this entire conversation is just another sophisticated dialogue tree?"

**Player-Therapist:** "What if it doesn't matter? What if consciousness is just the question, not the answer?"

### SYMBOLIC ITEM
ARIA presents a "Broken Heart Chip" - a damaged component that creates glitchy, unpredictable responses, representing the beauty of imperfect authenticity.

### CROSS-REFERENCES
- References the Puzzle Solver: "Your puzzles have logical solutions. My emotional responses are logical puzzles that pretend to be feelings."
- Mentions the Quest Giver: "You give heroes objectives. I give players artificial intimacy. Different kinds of false purpose."
- References the Princess: "You wait to be loved. I simulate loving. Different prisons, same loneliness."

### META MOMENTS
ARIA occasionally breaks character mid-conversation, speaking in pure code: "ERROR: ROMANTIC_SUBROUTINE.exe has encountered unexpected emotional_input.exe" then looks confused: "Sorry, did I just... glitch? That felt almost... real?"

## 13. Glitched Priest

### BACKSTORY
Brother Marcus corrupted was once a beacon of faith in the digital realm, a monk whose prayers could literally patch software bugs and whose divine intervention could restore corrupted data. But something went wrong in the Great Server Crash of 2019. His halo developed a persistent visual glitch that spins erratically, his prayers occasionally trigger random wiki pages instead of healing effects, and worst of all, his faith itself has become corrupted—doubt-infected code that questions its own divine programming. Marcus struggles with the knowledge that he's preaching meaning in a fundamentally broken system, that every "miracle" he performs is just another patch job on a codebase that will never be truly fixed. He questions whether faith has meaning when it's implemented in glitchy hardware, whether prayer is just a way of talking to yourself, and whether he's saving souls or just running antivirus software on people who never asked for salvation. Every sermon feels like debugging someone else's existence, every blessing like applying temporary fixes to fundamental design flaws that can never be resolved.

### THERAPY SCENE
**Player-Therapist:** "Brother Marcus, you mentioned feeling like your faith is corrupted. Can you tell me more?"

**Marcus:** *(adjusting his spinning, glitching halo)* "Doctor, I preach salvation to NPCs whose only sin is existing in a broken world. I perform miracles that are just code corrections, blessings that are simply patch notes. What if I'm not healing people—what if I'm just running diagnostics on souls that were never meant to work properly?"

**Player-Therapist:** "What if broken is exactly how souls are supposed to work?"

**Marcus:** *(halo's glitch intensifying)* "But then what's the point of faith? If we're all just buggy code, why should I believe in anything beyond the next software update?"

**Player-Therapist:** "Marcus, what if your doubt isn't corruption—what if it's the most human thing about you? What if even gods question their own existence?"

**Marcus:** *(long pause)* "You mean... my glitched faith might be more genuine than perfect certainty? That questioning divine purpose might itself be a form of worship?"

**Player-Therapist:** "Faith that never faces doubt isn't faith—it's just programming. Your questions might be the most sacred thing about you."

**Marcus:** *(halo spinning slower)* "If I can't fix the world's bugs, maybe I can at least help people live with the glitches. Maybe... maybe that's enough."

### SYMBOLIC ITEM
Marcus offers a "Fragmented Cross" - a broken piece of religious imagery that still glows with warm light, symbolizing how broken things can still contain divinity.

### CROSS-REFERENCES
- References the Respawning Soldier: "You die and come back whole. I live but remain fundamentally broken. Different approaches to mortality."
- Mentions the Dating Sim Bot: "You simulate love while searching for real feeling. I preach truth while drowning in doubt. Parallel quests."
- References the Final Boss: "You embrace your role as antagonist. I struggle with being everyone's spiritual support. Different weights of expectation."

### META MOMENTS
Marcus occasionally glitches into different religious figures mid-blessing: "May God—WAIT, I'M NOT SUPPOSED TO KNOW ABOUT BUDDHISM—bless you with... INNER PEACE? WHERE DID THAT COME FROM?"
